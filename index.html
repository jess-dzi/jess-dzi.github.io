About

I got into product management by asking the wrong questions at the right time.

At PwC, I was brought on to build Python automation for M&A contract analysis. Somewhere in that project, I realized I cared less about the code itself and more about what it changed: how a team reviewed contracts, how fast they could move, what became possible once the manual work disappeared. That's the part of the job I couldn't stop thinking about.

I studied Computer Science and Chinese Language at UVA, which taught me two very different ways of thinking about systems — one technical, one cultural. I've also spent time in cybersecurity, which sharpened how I think about risk and tradeoffs.

I'm currently in Qingdao, China, building my Mandarin fluency and watching how differently products get built here. I'm looking for a role where I can translate between engineers and the business, ask better questions, and ship things people actually use.
